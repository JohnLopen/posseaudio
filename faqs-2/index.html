Posse Audio \ FAQs
  • WHY DO I NEED POSSE?

    • POSSE was originally designed to correct for  monitoring problems that a performer can encounter on the stage and in the studio. By being able to independently set the level of your vocal mic, your instrument output, the  ambiance of the room and any auxiliary  input(s), you can customize your monitors to a comfortable and performance enhancing mix (you play better when you sound good to yourself). By carrying your mix with you from venue to venue, you'll know and look forward to what you're onstage monitor-mix is going to sound like at every show. (See our Applications page for all of the ways the POSSE can improve your performance environment)

  • HOW DO I ADD REVERB TO MY POSSE MIX?

    • The POSSE does not have a built in reverb but you can add some to your earphone mix by sending reverb ( triggered by your Vocal, Instrument or both) from the main mixing board to the AUX IN(s) on the POSSE Floor Box. Turning up the AUX knob on the Stand Box will add the effect to your earphones.

  • I SETUP EVERYTHING LIKE I WAS SUPPOSED TO AND NOW MY GUITAR IS NOT GOING TO THE HOUSE PA BOARD. WHAT'S UP?

    • If the blue TUNE light is illuminated on the Stand Box, then your guitar is in TUNE mode and is being muted to the House mixer. That's usually it, but if it's not, check for a bad cable, connection or dead pre-amp battery in your guitar. A weak battery in the POSSE Floor Box could also be causing the problem. Put in a fresh one or switch to the power supply (included).

  • I HAVE SOME EARBUDS THAT I REALLY LIKE. DO I HAVE TO USE THE ONES THAT CAME WITH POSSE?

    • Absolutely not. We included a set so that  POSSE would be fully functional right out of the box. We expect that a lot of you will use your favorite earbuds and have designed POSSE to accommodate  the diverse levels and impedances of the many earphones that we tested.

  • iF I HAVE A PROBLEM WITH THE CABLE THAT CONNECTS THE STAND BOX TO THE FLOOR, AM I OUT OF COMMISSION UNTIL YOU CAN SEND ME A NEW ONE?

    • No. We purposefully chose to use a cable that was readily available at any electronic supply outlet (Radio Shack, Fry's, Target, etc.).  The cable that you're asking about is an HDMI cable and any version of HDMI with the same plug design, will work. We made a similar decision choice regarding the cable that connects the Floor Box to the Belt Box. It is a standard 5-pin DIN cable also known as a MIDI cable. If you're out there on the road and the one you have disappears or gets run over, you can find replacements at any music store. Just make sure that all five pins are wired. Some of the very old MIDI cables had only three pins working.

  • WHAT IF I WANT TO USE THE ACOUSTIC MIC AND MY GUITAR'S PICK UP?

    • If your guitar pickup has a volume control you can easily mix the two. Turn down your guitar volume all the way; turn down the POSSE Master, all the way; plug the Acoustic Mic into the Stand Box; bring up the INST volume and  the MASTER volume to a comfortable level; Mix your guitar in with the Acoustic Mic, using the guitar pickup volume control. You may want to reset the INST level in your earphones once you find the blend that you like. The House mixer will get the same blend from the POSSE's INST OUT jack.

  • I TRIED TO RECORD A PERFORMANCE BUT IT CAME BACK WAY TOO COMPRESSED. IS THERE A FIX?

    • If your recorder has a choice of compressor settings ( usually referred to as “Conference” and “Voice”), choose “Voice”. It has the least amount of compression. We had great luck with the little Olympus series of hand held stereo recorders. If you have a recorder with input volume and other input controls, you might spend a little time with it at home to find the optimum setting for use with POSSE.

  • IF I USE A CONDENSOR MIX, AND I'M GETTING PHANTOM POWER FROM THE HOUSE MIXING CONSOLE, WILL IT HURT ANYTHING IF POSSE's PHANTOM POWER IS TURNED ON? …..AND WHEN WOULD I NEED POSSE's PHANTOM POWER?

    • It won't hurt anything. If you're using the battery, POSSE's internal phantom power will cause the battery to drain a little faster, that's all. In that case, if you have a reliable phantom power source coming from the house mixing board, you should turn the POSSE phantom power off. If you're not using a condenser mic that requires phantom power you should leave it off (default position). Some mics, especially ribbon types, can be harmed by the phantom power voltage. We added the phantom power option for those times that you get to a little club carrying your favorite condenser vocal mic to discover that the house mixing board doesn't have phantom power or it's not working..... and.... some small home studio's may not have an easy to use phantom power option. POSSE provides one.

  • I MOVE AROUND THE STAGE ALOT AND OCCASIONALLY I STEP ON THE 5-pin CABLE (MIDI) THAT GOES TO MY BELT BOX. HAVE YOU GOT A FIX FOR THAT?

    • There's a couple of things that will fix that. First try using a midi cable with a right angle plug at the belt box.  Now when you step on it, the cable will be tugged from the side, instead of straight down. The second fix is cheaper (you don't need to buy another MIDI cable). Wrap you guitar cord around the MIDI cable, use the velcro straps that are attached to the two cables to wrap around them - one just behind each end of the guitar cord. This will cause the MIDI cable to fall to the ground at the butt of the guitar (much like a regular guitar cord does). That method will eliminate the strain on the MIDI cable.
